Idea

Given a type A, a {{#concept "composition operation" Disambiguation="on binary families of sets" Agda=composition-operation-binary-family-Set}} on a binary family of sets hom : A ‚Üí A ‚Üí Set is a map

  _‚àò_ : hom y z ‚Üí hom x y ‚Üí hom x z

for every triple of elements x y z : A.

For such operations, we can consider properties such as associativity and unitality.

Being unital is a property of composition operations on binary families of sets

Proof: Suppose e e' : (x : A) ‚Üí hom-set x x are both right and left units with regard to composition. It is enough to show that e Ôºù e' since the right and left unit laws are propositions (because all hom-types are sets). By function extensionality, it is enough to show that e x Ôºù e' x for all x : A. But by the unit laws we have the following chain of equalities: e x Ôºù (e' x) ‚àò (e x) Ôºù e' x.
