Idea

Consider a family of elements b : (i : I) ‚Üí B i (a i) over a family of elements a : (i : I) ‚Üí A i and consider a family of types

  C : (i : I) (x : A i) ‚Üí B i x ‚Üí ùí∞.

Recall that b is also a dependent lift of the family of elements a. The type of {{#concept "dependent double lifts" Disambiguation="family of elements" Agda=dependent-double-lift-family-of-elements}} of b and a to C is defined to be the type

  (i : I) ‚Üí C i (a i) (b i).

Note that this is the type of double lifts in the sense that it simultaneously lifts a and b to the type family C.

The definition of (ordinary) double lifts is somewhat simpler: Given a lift b of a : I ‚Üí A to a type family B : A ‚Üí ùí∞, a {{#concept "double lift" Disambiguation="families of elements" Agda=double-lift-family-of-elements}} of a and b to a type family

  C : (x : A) ‚Üí B x ‚Üí ùí∞

is a family of elements

  (i : I) ‚Üí C (a i) (b i).

Note that this is the type of double lifts in the sense that it simultaneously lifts a and b to the type family C.

The type of double lifts plays a role in the universal property of the family of fibers of a map.
