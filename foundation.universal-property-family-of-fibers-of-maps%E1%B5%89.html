Idea

Any map f : A ‚Üí B induces a type family fiber f : B ‚Üí ùí∞ of fibers of f. By precomposing with f, we obtain the type family (fiber f) ‚àò f : A ‚Üí ùí∞, which always has a section given by

  Œª a ‚Üí (a , refl) : (a : A) ‚Üí fiber f (f a).

We can uniquely characterize the family of fibers fiber f : B ‚Üí ùí∞ as the initial type family equipped with such a section. Explicitly, the {{#concept "universal property of the family of fibers" Disambiguation="maps" Agda=universal-property-family-of-fibers}} fiber f : B ‚Üí ùí∞ of a map f is that the precomposition operation

  ((b : B) ‚Üí fiber f b ‚Üí X b) ‚Üí ((a : A) ‚Üí X (f a))

is an equivalence for any type family X : B ‚Üí ùí∞. Note that for any type family X over B and any map f : A ‚Üí B, the type of lifts of f to X is precisely the type of sections

  (a : A) ‚Üí X (f a).

The family of fibers of f is therefore the initial type family over B equipped with a lift of f.

This universal property is especially useful when A or B enjoy mapping out universal properties. This lets us characterize the sections (a : A) ‚Üí X (f a) in terms of the mapping out properties of A and the descent data of B.

Note: We disambiguate between the universal property of the family of fibers of a map and the universal property of the fiber of a map at a point in the codomain. The universal property of the family of fibers of a map is as described above, while the universal property of the fiber fiber f b of a map f at b is a special case of the universal property of pullbacks.

A type family C over B satisfies the universal property of the family of fibers of a map f : A ‚Üí B if and only if the constant map C b ‚Üí (fiber f b ‚Üí C b) is an equivalence for every b : B

In other words, the dependent type C is f-local if its fiber over b is fiber f b-null for every b : B.
